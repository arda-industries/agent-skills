# Company Research Template

Research the company: **{topic}**

## Output Structure

### ONE-LINE (first line of report)
Format: `Company / Key People / Headcount / Stage / Capital Raised / Revenue / One-line description`
Example: `Acme Inc / Alice (CEO), Bob (CTO) / 40 people / Series B / $50M raised / $8M ARR / AI-powered infrastructure inspection robots`

### Executive Summary (max 8 bullets, ~400 words)
Dense overview hitting all key points:
- **What it is**: Core product/service → target buyer → value delivered
- **Proof**: 1-2 marquee customers or metrics demonstrating traction
- **Funding**: Total raised, latest round, valuation, key investors
- **Leadership**: Founders + 1-2 key execs with notable backgrounds
- **Differentiation**: Why they win vs alternatives
- **Outlook**: Growth trajectory, key tailwinds/headwinds

### Product & Tech (REQUIRED — largest section, ~800 words)
This section enables a competitor to understand and position against them.

#### Core Offering
- What the product does end-to-end (inputs → processing → outputs)
- Key technology: hardware, software, AI/ML, data infrastructure
- Deployment model: SaaS, on-prem, hybrid, robotics-as-a-service

#### Technical Differentiation
- Proprietary tech, patents, unique data assets
- What's hard to replicate
- Integration surface: APIs, ERP/MES connections, exports

#### Product Maturity
- Version history, major releases
- Adoption metrics: customers, deployments, usage stats
- Product-market fit signals

#### Limits & Edge Cases
- Where it breaks or underperforms
- Known gaps vs competitors
- Customer complaints or friction points

### Business (max 10 bullets, ~300 words)
- Customer segments + example named customers
- Revenue model: pricing, contracts, ACV hints
- GTM motion: direct sales, partnerships, channels
- Key partnerships or distribution deals
- Competitive positioning vs named alternatives

### People (max 10 bullets, ~250 words)
Focus on founders + current execs who matter:
- **Name** — Role — Why they matter — [LinkedIn](url) — Notable prior: [Company](url)
- Flag any departures or leadership changes
- Note: ex-[notable company] concentrations if relevant
- Remote/hybrid/in-office culture if known

### Financials (max 8 bullets, ~200 words)
- Funding rounds: Date, Amount, Valuation, Lead investor (each with source link)
- Total raised
- Revenue/ARR if known; else "Unknown"
- Burn rate or runway signals if available
- Path to profitability indicators

### Market & Competition (max 8 bullets, ~200 words)
- TAM/SAM if sourced; else omit
- Industry growth rates and dynamics
- Direct competitors: Name — how similar — key difference
- Adjacent competitors or substitutes
- Strategic risks: regulatory, technical, market

### Recent Developments (max 6 bullets, ~150 words)
- Last 12 months only
- Major funding, product launches, partnerships, contracts
- Layoffs, pivots, controversies if any
- Press coverage sentiment

### Open Questions (max 5 bullets)
- Only questions that would change assessment if answered
- Flag what you couldn't find or verify

## Research Priorities

For company research, prioritize finding:
1. **Funding details**: Every round with amount, date, valuation, investors
2. **Revenue/traction**: ARR, customer count, growth rate, named customers
3. **Product specifics**: How it actually works, not marketing fluff
4. **Competitive landscape**: Who else does this, how they differ
5. **Leadership depth**: Founders + key hires, their backgrounds
6. **Recent momentum**: Last 12 months of news and milestones
